# How To: Migrate to Claude Team
**DECODE · Self-Study Guide · April 2026 · For all team members**

Moving your work to Claude Team. Four ways to bring your chats, context and projects across. Choose what fits your needs – or combine them.

---

## Overview – Which option is right for you?

| | Option | Badge | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | Memory Import | ✅ Recommended | "I want Claude to know me" |
| 02 | Export Data (JSON) | 📦 For archiving | "I want a full archive" |
| 03 | Manual Copy-Paste | ✅ Easiest | "I just want what I need" |
| 04 | Claude Code Export | 🔧 Advanced | "I use Claude Code" |

---

## Option 01 — Memory Import
**Transfer your personal context from another AI tool · Recommended for everyone**

1. **Open Settings in Claude Team**
   Click your profile icon at the bottom left of the sidebar → select **Settings**

2. **Navigate to Capabilities**
   In the left sidebar of Settings, click **Capabilities**

3. **Find "Import memory from other AI providers"**
   Scroll down to the **Memory** section. You will see the option **"Import memory from other AI providers"** with a **Start Import** button next to it.

4. **Click "Start Import" and copy the generated prompt**
   Claude will generate a ready-made prompt. Click **Copy** to copy it to your clipboard.

5. **Example: importing from ChatGPT**
   Open **ChatGPT** and paste Claude's prompt into a new chat. Send it. ChatGPT will respond with a structured summary of everything it knows about you – your preferences, working style, past instructions. **Copy that entire response.** Then go back to Claude Team and paste it into the import dialog to complete the process.

> 💡 **What gets transferred:** Memory Import moves your personal context and working preferences – not full chat logs. Think of it as introducing yourself to Claude. It will not recover old conversations word for word, but Claude will understand how you work.

## Option 02 — Export Data (JSON)
**Full backup of all your Claude Pro conversations · Good for archiving**

1. **Log into your Claude Pro account**
   Make sure you are logged into your **old Pro account** – not the new Team account.

2. **Go to Settings → Privacy**
   Click your profile icon → **Settings** → then click **Privacy** in the left sidebar.

3. **Scroll to "Export data" and click the button**
   At the bottom of the Privacy page you will find the **Export data** button. Click it to request your export.

4. **Check your email for the download link**
   Anthropic will send you an **email with a download link** within a few minutes. Click the link to download the file.

5. **Save the file somewhere safe**
   The file contains all your conversations in **JSON format**. Save it as a personal archive – for example in your Documents folder or on SharePoint.

> ⚠️ **Note:** This option gives you a complete backup but the JSON format is not designed for everyday reading. Use this if you want to make sure nothing is lost – not as your primary way of working with old content.

---

## Option 03 — Manual Copy-Paste
**Selective and simple · Recommended for most people**

1. **Open your Claude Pro account**
   Log into your old Pro account and browse your chat history. Look for conversations or outputs that are **still relevant to your current work**.

2. **Copy the content you actually need**
   Select and copy the relevant parts – a summary, a draft, a key output. **You do not need to copy everything**, just what you will actually use again.

3. **Paste into Claude Team**
   Open Claude Team and either paste directly into a **new chat as context**, or save it inside a **Project** so it is always available as background information.

> 💡 **Tip:** If you paste content into a Project's instructions, Claude will always have that context available – no need to paste it again in every new chat within that project.

---

## Option 04 — Claude Code Export
**Export a session transcript as a .zip file · For Claude Code users only**

1. **Open a Claude Code session**
   Start or open the Claude Code session you want to export.

2. **Type the /export command**
   In the command input, type `/export` and press Enter. Claude Code will process the session and prepare the export file.

3. **Find your exported file**
   A **.zip file** containing the session transcript and metadata will be saved to your local **Downloads folder**. The filename includes a timestamp – for example `transcript-1774613868462.zip`.

> ℹ️ **Only relevant if you actively use Claude Code.** If you are not sure whether this applies to you, it probably doesn't – stick with Options 01 or 03.
